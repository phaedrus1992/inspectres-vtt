# Weird Agents

Some investigators are not ordinary humans. **Weird Agents** are paranormal beings with supernatural powers and unique mechanics. Instead of a standard agent, you can play as a Psychic, Ghost, Werewolf, Vampire, or other supernatural archetype.

## What Makes a Weird Agent

**Weird Agents** have:
- **Powers** — Supernatural abilities tied to a specific skill (e.g., Telepathy uses Contact)
- **Cool Pool** — A pool of **Cool** points (0-5) spent to activate powers
- **Characteristics** — Unique quirks, vulnerabilities, or restrictions (e.g., vampires cannot enter sanctified ground)
- **Skill variations** — Some archetypes trade athletic ability for mental or social prowess

Unlike regular agents, Weird Agents integrate their supernatural nature into investigations. A Psychic reads minds; a Ghost phases through walls; a Werewolf tracks by scent.

## Power System

### Activating a Power

Powers are activated in two ways:

**Without Cool cost:** Use the power as part of a normal skill roll. Example: A Psychic reads surface thoughts as part of a Contact roll to intimidate a suspect. No extra cost.

**With Cool cost:** Spend **Cool** to unlock special effects or override normal limits. Example: A Psychic spends 1 Cool to read surface thoughts without the target's consent (normally not allowed).

### Cool Pool

Each Weird Agent starts with **Cool points** (0-5, set by archetype). Cool is a finite resource:
- **Use:** Spend 1 Cool per power activation (varies by power)
- **Refresh:** Gain Cool during **Vacation** (franchise recovers, agents reset to full Cool)
- **Depletion:** If Cool reaches 0, you cannot activate powers; you play like a regular agent until vacation

### Power Base Skills

Each power is tied to a **Base Skill** (Academics, Athletics, Technology, or Contact). When you roll the base skill, you can invoke the power to enhance or modify the outcome.

Example:
- **Telepathy** (Contact-based): Roll Contact to communicate with a target, then use Telepathy to read their thoughts
- **Keen Senses** (Contact-based): Roll Contact for perception, gain +2 dice when tracking by scent

## Available Archetypes

### Psychic

A mentally-gifted investigator with telepathic and precognitive abilities.

**Skills:** Academics 2, Athletics 1, Technology 2, Contact 5  
**Cool Pool:** 0 (requires vacation to restore)

**Powers:**
- **Telepathy** (Contact, 1 Cool): Communicate silently with one target within line of sight. Read surface thoughts without consent (costs 1 Cool).
- **Precognition** (Contact, 1 Cool): See glimpses of a near-future event. Gain +2 dice on the next roll against that threat.

**Characteristics:**
- Prone to migraines from mental strain
- Caffeine-dependent (2+ cups per day)
- Takes stress penalties from crowds and loud environments

**Design Notes:** High Contact reflects mental abilities; low Athletics reflects physical fragility. High contact skill means psychics excel at social investigation but struggle with physical confrontation.

### Ghost

An incorporeal spirit bound to investigate the paranormal.

**Skills:** Academics 3, Athletics 0, Technology 0, Contact 2  
**Cool Pool:** 5 (highest of the archetypes)

**Powers:**
- **Phase Through Walls** (Athletics, 1 Cool): Pass through solid matter. Cannot bring physical objects with you (except your anchor).
- **Manifest** (Contact, 1 Cool): Become partially visible and tangible. Spend Cool to interact with physical objects (1 minute per die spent).

**Characteristics:**
- Incorporeal: cannot be harmed by physical attacks
- Bound to location/object: cannot stray more than 1 mile away
- Spending Cool allows brief physical interaction

**Design Notes:** Ghosts have high Academics (haunting knowledge), zero Athletics (incorporeal), and must spend Cool to interact physically. The 1-mile binding creates location-based constraints for missions.

### Werewolf

A dual-natured investigator with human and beast forms.

**Skills:** Academics 2, Athletics 2, Technology 2, Contact 1  
**Cool Pool:** 3

**Powers:**
- **Keen Senses** (Contact, 1 Cool): In beast form, gain +2 dice to Contact-based perception rolls. Track targets by scent over vast distances.
- **Ferocity** (Athletics, 1 Cool): In beast form, add +2 dice to Athletics-based attacks. Ignore pain thresholds.

**Characteristics:**
- Beast form stats: Athletics 7, Contact 0
- Vulnerable to wolfsbane and silver
- Cannot control transformation during full moon

**Design Notes:** Werewolves shift between human and beast forms, with form-specific skills. The vulnerability to silver/wolfsbane and full moon loss of control create mechanical and narrative constraints.

### Vampire

A creature of the night with shapeshifting and mind-control abilities.

**Skills:** Academics 2, Athletics 3, Technology 0, Contact 2  
**Cool Pool:** 3

**Powers:**
- **Shapeshifting** (Academics, 1 Cool): Assume the appearance of a specific humanoid. Lasts until you dismiss the form or lose Cool.
- **Mind Control** (Contact, 1 Cool): Command one humanoid target to perform a simple action. They can resist with a Contact roll if they're aware of the attempt.

**Characteristics:**
- Cannot enter sanctified ground (churches, temples, graves)
- Must feed on blood (1 per week or gain stress)
- Weaknesses: garlic, holy water, sunlight (take damage if exposed 1+ round)

**Design Notes:** Vampires are excellent infiltrators (Shapeshifting) and manipulators (Mind Control). The feeding requirement and sanctified ground restriction create ongoing complications.

## Playing a Weird Agent

### Mechanical Integration

Weird Agents use the same **Skill Rolls**, **Stress system**, and **Recovery** mechanics as regular agents. The difference is *how* they accomplish goals:
- A regular agent climbs a wall (Athletics roll)
- A Ghost phases through the wall (phase power, costs 1 Cool)
- A Werewolf climbs faster in beast form (Keen Senses/Ferocity)

### Narrative Integration

Your archetype informs how you investigate and interact with the world:
- Psychics **read minds** instead of interrogating
- Ghosts **observe locations unseen** but cannot touch anything without spending Cool
- Werewolves **track and hunt** with superior senses
- Vampires **infiltrate and manipulate** through supernatural persuasion

### Stress & Recovery

Weird Agents stress the same way as regular agents, but recovery may be narrative-specific:
- A Psychic might recover from migraines by resting alone
- A Ghost might be trapped or weakened by holy objects
- A Werewolf might struggle during the full moon
- A Vampire might hide from sunlight during recovery

GMs should play up archetype flavor during downtime.

### Cool Management

Cool is a primary resource. Decisions matter:
- Spend Cool now to bypass a challenge, or conserve for later crises?
- Risk a skill roll without the power, or burn Cool for guaranteed success?
- When does **Vacation** arrive to refresh your power pool?

## Creating a Weird Agent

To add a Weird Agent to your campaign:

1. **Choose an archetype** (or work with your GM to design a custom one)
2. **Check your Cool pool** — You start with the archetype's Cool (0-5)
3. **Review your characteristics** — These are mechanical and narrative; plan for complications
4. **Note your power base skills** — These determine when you can invoke powers
5. **Plan your archetype's role** — How does your nature change investigation strategy?

## Custom Weird Agents

The archetypes provided are starting points. You and your GM can design custom Weird Agents:
- **New powers:** What can this being do that a human cannot?
- **New skills:** Does the archetype trade skills differently?
- **New characteristics:** What makes this archetype vulnerable or restricted?
- **New Cool pool:** Does this archetype recover Cool differently?

See the rules spec or ask your GM for guidance on balancing custom archetypes.

## FAQ: Weird Agents

**Q: Can I start as a regular agent and become weird later?**  
A: Discuss with your GM. Becoming weird is a major narrative moment—it should be earned through the story, not just a mechanical choice.

**Q: What if my power doesn't match my skill?**  
A: Powers are tied to skills, but the fiction can stretch it. Example: A Psychic using Telepathy to sense emotions (Contact-based power used intuitively without a roll). Always check with your GM.

**Q: What happens if I run out of Cool?**  
A: You cannot activate powers until **Vacation** restores Cool. You play as a regular agent with your archetype's normal skill rolls.

**Q: Can multiple Weird Agents be in the same franchise?**  
A: Yes. Each brings unique abilities and complications. The GM should balance power levels so all agents feel useful.

**Q: Do Weird Agents cost more to field than regular agents?**  
A: Not mechanically. They use the same resource rules. However, narrative complications (ghosts bound to locations, vampires needing feeding, etc.) may require GM accommodation.
