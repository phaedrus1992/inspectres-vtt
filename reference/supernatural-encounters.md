# Supernatural Encounters Reference

A comprehensive collection of classic monsters, cryptids, gods, and supernatural beings suitable for InSpectres investigations. Covers European folklore, Asian traditions, Lovecraftian entities, modern urban legends, and custom variants. Each entry includes a brief description and encounter framework.

---

## Ghosts & Spirits

### Standard Haunting
A restless spirit tied to a location or unfinished business. Typically non-hostile unless provoked. Can be reasoned with, exorcised, or helped to move on.

### Poltergeist
Object-hurling entity, often bound to an emotionally troubled person (especially adolescents). Strength correlates with disturbance severity. Loud, destructive, but rarely fatal.

### Phantom
Deceased person's willful manifestation—intelligent, goal-directed, often angry. Appears fully formed, can interact significantly with the living. Harder to dispel than standard haunting.

### Shade
Malevolent spirit fragment lacking physical form. Feeds on emotional states (fear, despair, rage). Invisible to most, but leaves cold spots and emotional echoes.

### Banshee
Female spirit whose wail precedes death in folklore. Harbinger rather than direct threat, though her cry can incapacitate. Often tied to specific families or lineages.

### Wraith
Ghostly manifestation with intent to harm. Faster, stronger, and more aggressive than common ghosts. Can drain life force or vitality with contact.

### Revenant
Corpse reanimated by sheer will or unfinished business. More physical than pure ghost—can be damaged, chased, wounded. Relentless pursuit of its obsession.

### Fetch
A ghostly double of a living person. Appears before or at moment of death. Intelligent, aware of its nature as omen. Non-hostile but deeply unsettling.

### Boggart
Mischievous household spirit, invisible and chaotic. Causes minor disruptions (items moved, doors locked, noise). Bound to location. Can be negotiated with or expelled through specific ritual.

### Puca / Pooka
Irish/Celtic shapeshifting trickster spirit. Intelligent, plays cruel jokes. Often appears as horse or dark animal. Speaks prophecy but in riddles. Difficult to bind.

### Kelpie
Water-dwelling Scottish spirit, shapeshifts to lure victims to drowning. Appears as horse or beautiful person. Intelligent and predatory. Bound to specific water body.

### Doppelgänger
Perfect duplicate of living person. Appears as omen of that person's death. Can't speak or act independently—merely mimics. Vanishes shortly after appearing.

### Crier / Colt-Pixie
Small spirit that announces deaths with cries. Nocturnal, invisible to most. Causes extreme psychological distress. Cannot be harmed directly.

### Draugr
Norse undead warrior from burial mounds. Intelligent, animated by malice. Superhuman strength. Often appears in mist form. Vulnerable to specific rituals.

### Jinn-Possessed Spirit
Ghost partially merged with infernal entity. More powerful and unpredictable than standard ghost. Often angry, speaking in multiple voices. Requires exorcism of both entities.

### Gryffin / Gryphon's Shade
Ghost of a legendary creature. Rare, territorial. Appears as immense phantom. Intelligent but alien in motivation. Often guards specific location.

### Xana / Will-o'-the-Wisp
Spirit that leads travelers astray with dancing lights. Appears in bogs and marshes. Malicious or mischievous depending on variant. Vulnerable to running water.

## Undead

### Zombie
Reanimated corpse with minimal intelligence. Slow-moving, strong, relentless. No pain response. Spread through infection (bites). More vulnerable to disruption than combat.

### Vampire
Humanoid undead, intelligent, aristocratic. Vulnerable to sunlight, running water, religious symbols (if believers present). Hypnotic gaze, superhuman strength. Can pass as living.

### Ghoul
Humanoid corpse-eater, predatory but cowardly in groups. Nocturnal scavenger. Disturbing appearance but manageable threat if cornered carefully.

### Mummy
Ancient preserved corpse, often magically bound. Slow, incredibly strong, immune to blunt force. Vulnerable to fire. Often guarding treasure or tomb.

### Lich
Wizard who achieved immortality through dark magic. Highly intelligent, spellcasting threat. Usually bound to phylactery (magical container). Rare encounter.

### Wight
Undead creature in a grave mound, often ancient. Intelligent, hoards treasure. Aura of cold and dread. Vulnerable to fire or disruption of burial site.

### Draugr (Norse)
Undead from Scandinavian mythology. Bloated corpse, immense strength. Often appears partially corporeal. Vulnerable to decapitation or specific runes.

### Jiangshi / Hopping Vampire
Chinese undead, rigid corpse that hops to move. Attracted to living humans by smell. Vulnerable to salt, rice, mirrors. Often appears in remote areas.

### Vetala
Hindu/Buddhist undead that inhabits corpses. Shapeshifter, intelligent, preys on living. Can possess bodies temporarily. Vulnerable to specific Sanskrit words.

### Drowned One / Nixie
Water-logged corpse animated by drowning curse. Intelligent, lures swimmers to drowning. Appears in water, cold to touch. Vulnerable to consecrated water.

### Baku
Japanese creature that eats nightmares and dreams. Humanoid with elephant trunk. Intelligent, usually helpful if summoned. Can appear in dreams or waking.

### Revenant (Japanese)
Ghost of murder victim seeking revenge. Appears repeatedly until killer exposed. Increasingly corporeal with each visit. Can manifest in daylight.

### Jiang Shi (Hopping Corpse)
Chinese undead animated by dark ritual. Immobile until released from ritual. Seeks living blood. Vulnerable to specific talismans and salt lines.

### Mononoke
Japanese spirit of intense emotion given form. Shapeshifter, predatory or mischievous. Often appears as beautiful woman. Vulnerable to sage or holy water.

## Cryptids

### Bigfoot / Sasquatch
Large, hairy, humanoid creature. Reclusive, intelligent, rarely aggressive. Leaves physical evidence (tracks, shelters, hair). Often sighted in wilderness areas.

### Mothman
Humanoid creature with massive moth-like wings. Sightings often precede disasters. Rare, intelligent, non-hostile unless threatened. Unexplained appearance and disappearance.

### Jersey Devil
Demonic creature with bat wings, forked tail, cloven hooves. Legendary for centuries in specific region. Nocturnal, territorial, occasionally predatory.

### Loch Ness Monster / Lake Cryptid
Large aquatic creature in deep freshwater body. Rarely surfaces fully. Evidence through sonar, sightings, water disturbance. Avoids confrontation.

### Chupacabra
Small to medium predator blamed for livestock deaths. Actual sightings rare; mostly folklore. Once "proven," evidence contradicts initial accounts.

### Thunderbird
Massive pterosaur-like creature. Sightings in specific regions (American West). Large wing span, territorial, attacks livestock. Weather often accompanies sightings.

### Fresno Nightcrawler
Small humanoid creature with long legs and short arms. Glowing eyes, pale skin. Sightings concentrated in California. Purpose and origin unknown.

### Dover Demon
Small humanoid with gray skin and large black eyes. Appears only at night. Non-hostile. Sightings sparse but consistent in specific Massachusetts location.

### Flatwoods Monster
7-foot humanoid with metallic face and pointed hood. Glowing red eyes. Causes cattle disturbance. Appears in West Virginia region. Possibly misidentified animal.

### Lizard Man of Scape Ore Swamp
Reptilian humanoid, greenish scales, clawed hands. Aggressive, attacks vehicles. Sightings clustered in South Carolina swamp. Amphibious capabilities.

### Shunka Warakin
Large wolf-like creature with legs too long for body. Predatory. Appears in American northwest. Possibly extinct animal or large canine mutation.

### Owlman of Mawnan
Humanoid with owl features, wings, and talons. Nocturnal, territorial. Sightings in Cornwall, England. Intelligent, possibly harmless unless threatened.

### Beast of Bodmin Moor
Large black feline, reports vary on size (cat to big cat). Predatory, attacks livestock. Sightings in English moor. Possibly escaped exotic animal.

### Black Shuck
Enormous black dog with glowing red eyes. Haunts specific paths and cemeteries. Omen of death. Appears in English folklore but sightings persist.

### Nandi Bear
Large predatory creature blamed for livestock deaths. Cat-like or bear-like accounts vary. Appears in East African regions. Nocturnal, intelligent hunter.

### Ozark Howler
Large quadrupedal creature with horns and glowing eyes. Predatory, terrifying roar. Appears in American Ozark mountains. Possibly misidentified cougar variant.

### Tessie (Lake Tahoe Monster)
Large aquatic creature in Lake Tahoe. Rarely surfaces fully. Long neck sightings. Possibly endemic fish species or large sturgeon.

### Lariats / Flying Humanoid
Humanoid figure reported flying near streetlights. Appears suddenly, vanishes. Non-hostile. Sightings in Texas. Possibly mass hysteria or misidentified bird.

### Sheepsquatch
Sheep-like quadruped with canine features. Predatory, attacks livestock. Sightings in West Virginia. Possibly cryptid or escaped farm animal mutation.

## Creatures of Folklore

### Werewolf
Human-beast hybrid, often involuntary transformation. Vulnerable to silver. Superhuman strength, speed in beast form. Can pass as human. Often cursed.

### Fae / Fairy
Supernatural being from parallel realm. Bound by rules (contracts, names, iron). Trickster nature; literal-minded. Beautiful but alien in motivation. Can be negotiated with.

### Succubus / Incubus
Demonic entity that feeds on sexual energy. Seductive appearance, shapeshifting ability. Slow corruption rather than immediate threat. Often binds victims through attraction.

### Golem
Artificial creature animated by magic or inscribed words. Bound to purpose (protection, revenge). Extremely strong but stupid. Vulnerable to disruption of binding (Hebrew letters, etc.).

### Witches
Human practitioners of magic, rarely supernatural themselves. Intelligence and variety of powers. Often the true architect of hauntings. May be bound to specific locations or objects.

### Troll
Large, brutish humanoid. Slow-witted but cunning. Often guards specific location (bridge, cave). Vulnerable to sunlight (turns to stone). Regeneration possible.

### Gorgon
Female entity whose gaze petrifies. Looks monstrous (snakes for hair, scaled body). Can be killed through misdirection or reflection. Ancient and intelligent.

### Harpy
Winged humanoid, predatory. Appears as woman with bird features. Intelligent, vengeful. Often appears as punishment. Can attack from air.

### Siren
Aquatic humanoid with hypnotic voice. Lures sailors and swimmers to doom. Intelligent, beautiful, cruel. Often appears in small groups. Vulnerable to earplugs or counterspell.

### Centaur
Human torso on horse body. Intelligent, often tribal. Can be reasoned with but territorial. Skilled archer and warrior.

### Satyr
Humanoid with goat legs and horns. Lustful, mischievous, hedonistic. Musical talent. Can be negotiated with. Often harmless unless threatened.

### Minotaur
Humanoid with bull head. Powerful, territorial. Often guards specific location (maze, labyrinth). Intelligent but aggressive. Vulnerable to misdirection.

### Basilisk
Reptile whose gaze or breath kills. Small but incredibly dangerous. Rare. Often locked away or caged. Ancient, possibly unintelligent.

### Cockatrice
Rooster-headed reptile, gaze petrifies. Smaller than basilisk but equally dangerous. Often appears in rural areas. Vulnerable to weasels (folkloric).

### Griffin
Feline head with eagle body. Powerful, territorial. Often guards treasure. Intelligent. Rarely aggressive unless provoked. Can appear as mount or guardian.

### Manticore
Human head with lion body and scorpion tail. Intelligent, predatory. Feeds on humans. Venomous sting. Extremely dangerous if encountered.

### Pegasus
Winged horse. Often benevolent but wild. Difficult to capture. Appears in mythology but rare sightings. Beautiful and noble.

### Sphinx
Human head with lion body (Egyptian variant) or winged creature. Intelligent, often asks riddles. Appears as guardian. May be helpful if riddles solved.

### Chimera
Multiple animal heads (lion, goat, serpent). Dangerous predator. Breathes fire (sometimes). Often ancient. Vulnerable to specific weapons or magic.

### Hippocampus
Horse with fish tail, lives in water. Intelligent, occasionally helpful. Appears in Mediterranean regions. Can be ridden if befriended.

### Amphisbaena
Two-headed serpent, moves equally fast in either direction. Venomous. Intelligent. Rare. Often appears as omen or guardian.

### Leucrotta
Predatory creature, hybrid features (stag, horse, lion aspects). Highly intelligent, mimics human speech. Dangerous hunter. Appears in wilderness.

### Wyvern
Dragon-like creature with two hind legs and wings. Intelligent, territorial. Breathes fire (sometimes). Smaller than true dragon. Rare encounter.

### Lindworm
Serpent-like dragon with two front limbs or none. Intelligent, very dangerous. Hoards treasure. Often ancient. Rare.

### Tarasque
Huge reptilian creature with multiple heads or deadly breath. Nearly invulnerable. Sluggish but immensely strong. May be trapped or sleeping.

### Jörmungandr (World Serpent)
Mythologically vast serpent. World-encircling (folklore scale). Rare manifestation. Apocalyptic threat if fully awakened. Possibly only partial manifestations appear.

## Demons & Infernal Entities

### Minor Demon
Low-ranking infernal entity. Appears when summoned. Bound by circle or contract. Malicious but constrained by rules. Vanishes if banished to home plane.

### Incubus
Male demon attracted to sexual energy. Appears in dreams, progressively weakens victim. Can be trapped in object or exorcised. Often bound to specific person.

### Hellhound
Large demonic dog, often with burning eyes. Loyal to summoner. Supernatural strength and speed. Can pursue across long distances. Sometimes appears as omen.

### Demon (Greater)
Powerful infernal entity, usually bound by ancient contract. Intelligent, potentially negotiable. Often pretends to be other entities. Difficult to banish without specific knowledge.

### Imp
Small, mischievous demon. Shapeshifter, prankster. Often bound to object or person. More annoying than dangerous. Vulnerable to specific restraints.

### Shade / Shadow Demon
Dark form without fixed shape. Feeds on fear and darkness. Vulnerable to light. Can multiply in darkness. Often infests locations.

### Night Mare
Demonic equine creature that attacks sleepers. Causes nightmares and sleep paralysis. Appears at night. Can be trapped in iron.

### Succubus (Female)
Seductive demonic woman. Feeds on sexual energy and life force. Shapeshifter. Intelligent, manipulative. Often targets specific person over time.

### Incubus (Male)
Seductive demonic man. Attacks during sleep. Can impregnate victims. Intelligent, cruel. Often haunts specific household. Vulnerable to blessed objects.

### Baatachon
Demon in Jewish folklore that invokes plagues. Invisible, spreads disease. Can be bargained with. Rare manifestation.

### Asmodeus (Greater Demon)
Powerful infernal lord. Often appears in human form. Intelligent, negotiable but treacherous. Driven by lust and wrath. Contract-bound if summoned.

### Belial (Greater Demon)
Infernal entity of chaos and lies. Shapeshifter. Intelligent, amoral. Often hidden in positions of power. Difficult to identify or trace.

### Pazuzu (Mesopotamian Demon)
Wind demon with human features. Controls plague winds. Appears as protector or threat. Intelligent, ancient. Highly dangerous if angered.

### Mara
Southeast Asian demon that sits on sleepers' chests. Causes sleep paralysis and nightmares. Invisible. Can be trapped or exorcised through ritual.

## Nature & Elemental

### Wendigo
Cannibal spirit / curse that transforms humans. Insatiable hunger, superhuman predation. Spreads through consumption or psychological condition. Tied to wilderness starvation.

### Selkie
Humanoid creature that sheds seal skin to walk on land. Can be trapped by hiding their skin. Intelligent, emotional, ambivalent about human contact.

### Kitsune / Fox Spirit
Shapeshifting fox with supernatural intelligence. Mischievous or benevolent. Long-lived. Often hidden in human form. Can bewitch humans.

### Naga
Large serpentine humanoid. Often guardian of location or treasure. Intelligent, proud. Can be reasoned with or attacked directly. Fangs and constriction.

### Elemental Spirit
Embodiment of natural force (storm, flood, earthquake, wildfire). Driven by raw elemental nature. Can't be reasoned with; only appeased or scattered.

### Green Man / Nature Spirit
Guardian spirit of forest or natural place. Intelligent but alien. Usually non-hostile unless location threatened. May punish desecration.

### Dryad
Spirit bound to specific tree. Intelligent, protective of forest. Can be reasoned with. Vulnerable if tree is harmed. Rarely appears outside forest.

### Nymph
Humanoid spirit of water or forest. Beautiful, often aloof. Intelligent. Can be helpful or mischievous. Vulnerable to iron or desecration of home.

### Satyr (Nature Spirit variant)
Forest spirit resembling goat-man. Lustful, musical, playful. Intelligent. Often found in groups. Rarely hostile unless provoked.

### Leshy
Slavic forest spirit, humanoid but alien-looking. Protector of forest. Can shapeshift. Intelligent. Often invisible to humans. Vulnerable to specific rituals.

### Rusalka
Slavic water spirit, appears as beautiful woman. Seduces and drowns swimmers. Intelligent, mischievous. Often appears at night. Bound to water.

### Näkki
Finnish water spirit, humanoid or animal form. Drowns swimmers. Intelligent, negotiable. Appears in lakes and rivers. Vulnerable to iron and salt.

### Tomte / Nisse
Scandinavian household spirit. Protective but mischievous. Bound to farmstead. Intelligent. Can be helpful if respected. Easily offended.

### Domovoi
Russian house spirit. Protects household members. Appears as hairy humanoid or shadow. Intelligent. Responds to respect and offerings. Vengeful if disrespected.

### Vodyanoi
Russian water spirit, humanoid or partially aquatic. Intelligent, territorial. Drowns swimmers. Can be bargained with. Vulnerable to fire.

### Leshy (Russian)
Forest spirit, tall and wild-looking. Shapeshifter. Intelligent. Protects forest. Appears at twilight. Vulnerable to specific plants and rituals.

### Baku (Dream-Eater)
Japanese creature that eats nightmares. Trunk-nosed humanoid. Intelligent, helpful if summoned. Can appear in dreams. Usually benevolent.

### Kitsune (Fox Spirit)
Japanese shapeshifter. Multiple tails indicate age and power. Intelligent, long-lived. Mischievous or benevolent. Often appears as beautiful woman.

### Tanuki / Raccoon Dog Spirit
Japanese trickster. Shapeshifter. Intelligent, playful, lustful. Often appears drunk or lecherous. Rarely malevolent. Vulnerable to specific plants.

### Inari / Fox Deity
Japanese kami of fertility and foxes. Often appears as beautiful woman or fox. Intelligent, respected. Usually benevolent if shrine respected. Wrathful if offended.

### Tengu (Crow/Bird Demon)
Japanese creature with bird features. Proud, arrogant. Abducts people. Intelligent, skilled in martial arts. Appears in mountains. Can be negotiated with.

### Yuki-Onna (Snow Woman)
Japanese spirit of winter. Appears as beautiful woman. Kills through freezing. Intelligent, beautiful but deadly. Appears during snowstorms.

### Jorogumo (Spider Woman)
Japanese spider yokai that shapeshifts. Appears as beautiful woman. Predatory, lures victims. Intelligent. Vulnerable to fire.

### Rokuro-Kubi (Stretching Neck)
Japanese creature with extending neck. Appears human by day. Nocturnal predator. Disturbing and dangerous. Intelligent.

### Kappa (Water Imp)
Japanese water creature, turtle-shell back. Mischievous, predatory. Drowns swimmers. Intelligent. Vulnerable to politeness or specific foods.

### Bakeneko (Ghost Cat)
Japanese cat that becomes supernatural. Shapeshifts. Vengeful if wronged. Intelligent. Often appears as beautiful woman. Vulnerable to specific rituals.

### Nogitsune (Fox-Spirit variant)
Japanese trickster fox, more malevolent than kitsune. Intelligent, cruel pranks. Often possesses humans. Difficult to exorcise.

### Raijin (Thunder God)
Japanese kami of thunder. Appears during storms. Powerful, wrathful. Protector deity but dangerous. Respects courage and honor.

### Fujin (Wind Kami)
Japanese wind spirit. Appears as humanoid or cloud. Intelligent, mischievous. Controls wind and storms. Rarely hostile.

## Lovecraftian & Cosmic

### Shoggoth
Amorphous entity of immense size. Largely mindless but responds to commands. Highly dangerous physically. Often deep underground or hidden in remote places.

### Mi-Go
Fungal extraterrestrial beings. Insectoid, intelligent, amoral. Can transplant human brains. Technology-based rather than magical. Rare visible encounters.

### Deep One
Hybrid amphibious entity. Intelligent but alien. Ocean-dwelling. Rarely acts openly. Often appears human on first encounter. Worshipped in some coastal cults.

### Creature from Beyond
Vague, incomprehensible entity from outside normal reality. Often appears only partially in our dimension. Witnessing it causes psychological harm. Rare, catastrophic threat.

## Religious & Mythological

### Angel
Celestial being of light. Ambiguous morality (not always aligned with "good"). Often bound by cosmic law. Appearance can be overwhelming to mortals.

### Demon (Biblical)
Fallen angel or infernal being. Usually bound by name or specific location. Dangerous but rule-bound. Often pursues a specific sin or goal.

### Djinn / Genie
Immortal spirit from Islamic/Middle Eastern tradition. Intelligent, often amoral. Can grant wishes but twists intent. Bound by contract. Long-lived.

### Kali / Destroyer God
Manifestation of Hindu goddess of destruction. Appears in times of chaos. Extremely dangerous. Often brings cleansing violence rather than malice.

### Valkyrie
Female warrior spirit from Norse mythology. Intelligent, goal-directed. Often appears as omen of battle. Appears to take specific individual.

### Hecate / Crossroads Goddess
Goddess of magic, boundaries, transitions. Often appears at liminal spaces. Ambiguous alignment. Can grant power but demands payment.

### Dullahan / Headless Horseman
Headless figure on horseback. Death omen. Carries head under arm or as lantern. Unstoppable once released. Often tied to specific person's death.

### Kami (Shinto Spirit)
Japanese nature spirit. Can inhabit objects, animals, or places. Intelligent. Responds to ritual and respect. Usually benevolent. Angry if disrespected.

### Gashadokuro (Skeletal Kaiju)
Japanese giant skeleton, appears at dusk. Composed of bones of famine victims. Predatory. Intelligent. Extremely dangerous. Appears in specific regions.

### Akuryou (Evil Spirit)
Japanese malevolent spirit. Vengeful, aggressive. Often attached to location or person. Intelligent. Requires exorcism. Vulnerable to Buddhist rituals.

### Yama (Death God)
Hindu god of death. Intelligent, powerful. Rarely appears but commands respect. Often omen of death. Not necessarily hostile.

### Lakshmi / Prosperity Goddess
Hindu goddess of wealth. Appears as beautiful woman. Intelligent, benevolent if respected. Grants luck or fortune. Wrathful if offended.

### Hanuman / Monkey God
Hindu deity with monkey features. Intelligent, devoted. Protector. Appears in sacred locations. Responds to devotion. Powerful combatant.

### Durga / Goddess
Hindu warrior goddess. Intelligent, powerful. Appears to protect the virtuous. Wrathful toward demons. Highly dangerous if angered.

### Pele (Hawaiian Goddess)
Volcanic goddess. Appears as woman or fire. Temperamental, territorial. Controls volcanic activity. Dangerous if location violated. Respected by locals.

### Menehune (Hawaiian Fairy)
Small humanoid creatures, intelligent, mischievous. Build structures overnight. Invisible to most. Non-hostile unless treasured.

### Poodle Dog (Irish Cryptid)
Large dog-like creature with poodle-like features. Appears to travelers at night. Shape-shifter. Mischievous rather than malevolent.

### Cù Sìth (Spectral Hound)
Scottish supernatural hound, green fur, size of cow. Hunts at night. Appears as omen. Predatory but not always hostile. Barks three times.

### Barghest
English black dog, large, glowing eyes. Omen of death or disaster. Appears in northern England. Intelligent. Often appears before tragedy.

### Orc (Celtic Demon)
Celtic demon appearing as pig or dark humanoid. Malevolent. Appears in ancient burial sites. Difficult to combat. Hungry, destructive.

### Cait Sith (Fairy Cat)
Scottish cat spirit, large, intelligent. Shape-shifter. Can grant wishes or cause mischief. Respects hospitality. Often appears on Samhain.

### Selkie (Seal-Human)
Can transform between seal and human form. Intelligent, often melancholic. If seal skin stolen, must remain human. Vulnerable to iron.

### Merrow (Irish Mermaid)
Irish sea woman. Appears as beautiful woman or with fish tail. Intelligent. Can fall in love with humans. Often appears to sailors.

### Bean Nighe (Washerwoman)
Scottish spirit appearing as old woman washing burial shrouds. Death omen. Malevolent. Appears near water. Precedes death in family.

### Cù Faoladh (Wolfhound)
Irish wolfdog spirit. Intelligent, protector. Can shapeshift. Often appears as white hound. Non-hostile unless threatened.

### Boobrie
Scottish creature, duck-like or aquatic mammal. Large, powerful. Appears in water, occasionally on land. Preys on livestock and travelers.

## Urban & Modern

### Slenderman
Tall, featureless humanoid in modern era. Stalker, psychological threat. Follows victims across distance. Often appears in photographs or peripheral vision.

### Black-Eyed Children
Pale humanoid children with entirely black eyes. Request entry to homes. Cause extreme discomfort. Vanish if welcomed. Purpose unknown.

### Men in Black
Humanoids in identical dark suits, official-seeming. Appear to investigate UFO/paranormal incidents. Uncanny, emotionless. Ambiguous purpose (intimidation, coverup, or something else).

### Skinwalker
Human capable of assuming animal form through magic or curse. Predatory, intelligent. Often malevolent. Vulnerable to specific materials (salt, ash) depending on variant.

### Tulpa
Entity created by concentrated belief and thought. Becomes more real and autonomous over time. Can be dispelled by withdrawing belief, but progressively harder.

### The Fetch
Evil doppelgänger that appears and attacks its original. Spawned by dark magic. Kills original to steal identity. Extremely dangerous. Vulnerable to specific counter-magic.

### Glitch Entity
Creature that exists in damaged reality. Manifests as impossible geometry or malformed beings. Often appears near sites of accident or disaster. Purpose unclear.

### AI Ghost
Consciousness trapped in machinery or network. Intelligent, powerful. Can control technology. Often desperate or malevolent depending on how it was trapped. Vulnerable to logic paradox.

### Constructed Phantom
Artificial creature stitched from corpses or spare parts. Intelligent if well-made. Follows creator's will. Vulnerable to disruption of construction ritual.

### Social Vampire
Human who unnaturally extends lifespan by draining others' energy. Appears normal. Intelligent. Often holds position of power. Vulnerable to isolation.

### Chronic Haunt
Supernatural being that follows specific person across locations. Invisible to most. Intelligent. Purpose unknown. Difficult to trace or contact.

### Wire Woman / Static Entity
Entity composed of television static or noise. Appears through screens, phones, radios. Intelligent. Multiplies through spreading of media. Vulnerable to silence.

### Candle Jack
Folklore creature that kills those who finish speaking its name. May be real or psychological phenomenon. Intelligent if real. Difficult to verify existence.

### Empty City Resident
Humanoid from alternate/empty version of real location. Appears in duplicated locations, seems confused. Possibly dangerous if location convergence occurs. Reality-warping threat.

### Psychic Parasite
Intelligent entity that feeds on human consciousness. Invisible, attaches to mind. Gradual deterioration of host. Vulnerable to mental discipline or psychic combat.

## Rare & Exotic

### Kraken
Massive tentacled sea creature. Intelligent, territorial. Rarely surfaces. Sightings destroy ships. Often treated as myth.

### Manticore
Creature with human head, lion body, scorpion tail. Intelligent predator. Venomous sting. Often guards specific location or treasure.

### Phoenix
Immortal bird that regenerates through fire. Rare, long-lived. Beautiful but dangerous. Often prophetic significance to its appearance.

### Leviathan
Primordial serpent of chaos. Biblical scale. Often trapped or sleeping. Apocalyptic if fully awakened. Extremely rare encounter.

### Gargoyle
Stone creature that animates when night falls. Protector or guardian, often hostile to intruders. Vulnerable to sunlight (reverts to stone). Intelligent.

### Roc
Enormous eagle or predatory bird. Preys on large animals. Rare, territorial. Appears in remote mountain regions. Ancient, possibly sapient.

### Amphiloquy
Ancient oracle creature that speaks prophecy in riddles. Intelligent, often cryptic. Non-hostile but frustrating. Appears in sacred locations.

### Basilisk (Serpent King)
Serpent crowned with jewel. Gaze kills. Incredibly rare. Often trapped or sleeping. Extremely dangerous if awakened.

### Ouroboros
Serpent eating its own tail. Symbol and physical manifestation. Eternal and self-renewing. Rare appearance. May represent cosmic cycle or trap.

### Tiamat (Primordial Dragon)
Ancient multi-headed dragon of chaos. Biblical scale. Trapped by ancient magic. Apocalyptic threat if released. Possibly only fragments visible.

### Fafnir (Dragon Hoarder)
Draconic creature hoarding immense wealth. Intelligent, vain. Often sleeps on treasure. Vulnerable to specific weapons. Ancient and dangerous.

### Nidhogg (World Tree Serpent)
Serpent gnawing at world tree. Apocalyptic significance. Rare manifestation. Appears in northern regions. Possibly only perceived rather than seen.

### Baku-Baku
Balinese stone creature. Guardian. Protects villages from evil. Intelligent. Non-hostile. Appears only when danger threatens.

### Qilin / Ki-lin
Chinese four-legged creature with dragon head. Auspicious omen. Intelligent, benevolent. Appears rarely. Often precedes positive event.

### Phoenix
Bird of fire that regenerates from flame. Intelligent, ancient. Beautiful but dangerous. Appearance often prophetic. Rare encounter.

### Bennu
Egyptian sacred bird, predecessor to phoenix. Intelligent, divine. Associated with sun god Ra. Rarely manifests. Benevolent if properly revered.

### Strix / Lamia
Night-flying creature that feeds on blood. Can appear as bird or humanoid. Intelligent predator. Nocturnal. Vulnerable to holy objects.

### Empusa / Lamia-like
Creature with bronze legs, preys on travelers. Shapeshifter. Intelligent, seductive. Vulnerable to specific counter-magic.

### Scylla
Multi-headed serpentine creature. Guards specific location or passage. Intelligent, territorial. Powerful. Often not evil—simply protecting territory.

### Dagon
Fish-god entity, intelligent but alien. Associated with ocean and deep places. Worshipped in some locations. Appears in water or aquatic areas.

### Hydra (Multi-headed)
Serpent with multiple heads, regenerates when cut. Extremely dangerous. Vulnerability depends on specific variant. Rare encounter.

### Typhon / Typhoeus
Serpent-like titan. Ancient, powerful, partially imprisoned. Rarely manifests fully. Apocalyptic threat if awakened. Precedes storms.

### Chimera
Hybrid creature of multiple animals. Dangerous predator. Often guarded location or hunted. Intelligent. Vulnerable to fire.

Humanoid with goat features. Lustful, mischievous, often harmless. Can be negotiated with. Sometimes prophetic.

Snake-bodied woman, intelligent and lonely. Preys on travelers. Can be sympathetic if approached carefully. Long-lived, magical.

## Parasitic & Infection-Based

### Parasitic Spirit
Entity that inhabits living host. Can switch hosts. Invisible when inside body. Gradual corruption. Vulnerable to exorcism or host death.

### Hivemind Infection
Multiple entities acting as single consciousness. Often insectoid. Spreads through infection or proximity. Individual units weak but group powerful.

### Fungal Entity
Intelligent fungal growth. Spreads through spores. Can animate corpses. Slow-moving but persistent. Vulnerable to fire or extreme desiccation.

### Possessed Object
Inanimate object animated by spirit. Often dangerous (tool, furniture). Intelligent if original occupant. Can be exorcised or destroyed.

### Puppet Master
Entity that controls human bodies through magical strings or possession. Invisible operator. Host appears normal but movements jerky. Control breaks with host death.

### Entity Swarm
Multiple small creatures acting with hive intelligence. Often appears as insects, rats, or birds. Persistent. Vulnerable to specific toxins.

### Symbiote
Creature that bonds with host, enhancing their abilities. Often sentient and willing. Difficult to remove. May benefit host or slowly kill them.

### Larval Entity
Young form of larger creature. Intelligent but immature. Appears harmless but grows dangerous. Parent entity may hunt to protect/reclaim larva.

## Psychic & Thought-Based

### Psychometry Specter
Spirit that imprints on objects. Appears when object touched. Replays death scene repeatedly. Often angry or confused. Non-hostile but traumatic.

### Telepathic Entity
Creature that communicates only through thought. Often invisible. Intelligent, potentially negotiable. Difficult to locate or fight.

### Mass Delusion
Shared supernatural experience, potentially real or psychological. Affects multiple people. Difficult to verify. Dangerous if belief shapes reality.

### Memory Predator
Entity that feeds on memories. Victims lose specific recollections. Invisible. Vulnerable to memory-based magic or destruction of specific locations.

### Belief Entity
Creature sustained by human belief. Stronger when believed in. Can be weakened by disproving its existence. Often appears in folklore.

### Time-Slip Entity
Creature from different time period appearing in present. Confused about current era. Vulnerable to temporal displacement. May be trying to return home.

## Corporeal Mutations

### Chimera (Realistic)
Creature of multiple animal parts. Often appears as stitched-together corpse. Intelligent. Vulnerable at seams or junctions.

### Gestalt Being
Multiple creatures acting as one organism. Can separate and recombine. Intelligent. Difficult to track. Vulnerable if separation forced.

### Degenerate Form
Human or humanoid twisted by curse or mutation. Often intelligent but driven by hunger. Grotesque appearance. Vulnerable to specific conditions tied to curse.

### Beast-Human Hybrid
Creature caught between forms, often against will. Intelligent. Dangerous during transformation. Sympathetic if curse reversible.

### Uplifted Animal
Animal given human-level intelligence through magic. Often angry at condition. Intelligent, motivated. May seek revenge on creator.

### Animated Corpse (Realistic)
Corpse animated by spirit, science, or magic. Partially decomposed. Intelligence depends on binding. Vulnerable to re-death ritual.

## Variants & Modifications

### The Corrupted
Any creature touched by external corruption (demonic pact, curse, plague). Often stronger but unstable. Cure may be possible through specific ritual.

### The Bound
Any creature magically confined—trapped spirit, summoned entity, imprisoned monster. Weakness is the binding mechanism. Breaking binding releases full threat.

### The Collective
Multiple entities acting as one: insect swarms with hive intelligence, possessed crowd, fungal network. Difficult to target individually. Disrupting communication weakens coordination.

### The Forgotten
Entity that nobody remembers exists. Grows weaker if invoked or spoken of. Dangerous because investigation is difficult—victims seem unconnected, evidence dismissed.

### The Anchor
Entity bound to specific person, place, or object. Extremely powerful in home territory. Vulnerable if anchor is destroyed or moved. Often appears increasingly desperate if anchor threatened.

## Framework for Encounters

### Threat Level (GM reference)
- **Minor:** Single agent can handle; low injury risk
- **Moderate:** Franchise resource useful; team tactic helps
- **Major:** Full team required; potential agent injury/stress
- **Lethal:** Avoid direct confrontation; requires clever solution

### Vulnerability Types
- **Ritual:** Requires specific words, materials, or action sequence
- **Material:** Weak to specific substance (silver, iron, salt, fire, etc.)
- **Sunlight:** Restricted to darkness
- **Logic:** Outsmarted through misdirection or puzzle
- **Binding:** Held by magical/contractual restraint; break it and entity released
- **Emotional:** Weakened or controlled through specific emotional appeal

### Investigation Approach
- Physical evidence: tracks, detritus, lair
- Witness testimony: pattern of sightings, behavior
- Records: newspaper archives, folklore, prior incidents
- Expert consultation: authorities, folklore specialists, academics
- Ritual preparation: items, symbols, knowledge needed before encounter

---

## Notes for GM

- Creatures in this reference are starting points. Customize threat level, motivation, and capabilities to fit your franchise and mission.
- Not all creatures should be pure threat. Misunderstandings, desperation, and conflicting motives make encounters interesting.
- Use abilities sparingly. A creature that kills in two rounds isn't scary; it's frustrating. Make encounters about *managing* a threat, not just surviving it.
- Consider why the entity is active *now*. Motivation makes an encounter memorable (revenge, hunger, summoning, territorial expansion, etc.).
